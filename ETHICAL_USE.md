# Siyarix Ethical Use Policy

**Effective Date:** May 2026
**Version:** 1.0.0

Siyarix is an AI-powered cybersecurity platform designed to enhance defensive and authorized security testing capabilities. This policy defines acceptable and prohibited uses of the Siyarix platform, its components, and derivative works.

This policy applies to all users, contributors, distributors, and integrators of Siyarix, including any modified versions or services built upon it.

---

## 1. Permitted Use

You are authorized to use Siyarix for the following purposes, provided all applicable laws and contractual obligations are observed:

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Authorized Security Testing** | Penetration tests, vulnerability assessments, red-team exercises conducted with explicit written authorization |
| **Defensive Security** | Internal network monitoring, incident response, security hardening, compliance assessment |
| **Educational & Research** | Academic study, cybersecurity training, capture-the-flag (CTF) events, lab environments |
| **Bug Bounty Programs** | Testing within the scope of publicly disclosed bug bounty or vulnerability disclosure programs |
| **Malware Analysis** | Reverse engineering and analysis of malware in isolated, air-gapped environments |
| **Personal Learning** | Self-education in cybersecurity concepts, tool orchestration, and AI-assisted security workflows |
| **Internal Enterprise Use** | Security assessments of systems, networks, and applications you own or are authorized to test |

---

## 2. Prohibited Use

The following activities are **strictly prohibited** when using Siyarix or any of its components:

### 2.1 Unauthorized Access
- Scanning, probing, or attacking systems without explicit authorization
- Attempting to bypass access controls of systems you do not own
- Credential stuffing, password spraying, or brute-force attacks against unauthorized targets

### 2.2 Malicious Activities
- Deploying ransomware, malware, worms, trojans, or viruses
- Participating in botnet operations, DDoS attacks, or C2 infrastructure
- Engaging in cryptojacking or resource hijacking
- Developing or distributing exploit kits intended for illegal use

### 2.3 Social Engineering & Deception
- Conducting phishing or vishing campaigns without explicit authorization
- Creating or distributing deceptive communications intended to harm individuals or organizations
- Impersonation of individuals, organizations, or services for malicious purposes

### 2.4 Infrastructure Disruption
- Operations intended to disrupt, degrade, or destroy critical infrastructure
- Attacks on healthcare systems, emergency services, or essential public utilities
- Targeting industrial control systems (ICS/SCADA) without authorization

### 2.5 Illegal Surveillance
- Monitoring individuals without their knowledge or legal basis
- Unauthorized interception of communications
- Collection of personal data without lawful authority or consent

### 2.6 Privacy Violations
- Doxxing or publishing private information without consent
- Exfiltration of personally identifiable information (PII) without authorization
- Violation of data protection regulations (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)

### 2.7 Nation-State Abuse
- Use in offensive cyber operations conducted by or on behalf of any nation-state without lawful international mandate
- Development of cyber weapons targeting civilian infrastructure

### 2.8 Weaponization
- Modifying Siyarix for the purpose of causing physical harm, property damage, or loss of life
- Integration with weapons systems or military-grade offensive cyber capabilities without appropriate legal oversight

---

## 3. Authorized Use Requirements

When using Siyarix for permitted security testing:

1. **Written Authorization** — Always obtain explicit written permission before testing any system you do not own.
2. **Scope Boundaries** — Do not exceed the authorized scope of testing engagements.
3. **Data Handling** — Securely handle and promptly destroy any data collected during testing.
4. **Reporting** — Report findings to system owners in a timely, responsible manner.
5. **Least Privilege** — Use the minimum access and tooling necessary to accomplish the authorized objective.

---

## 4. Responsible Disclosure

If you discover a security vulnerability in Siyarix itself:

- Do **not** open a public GitHub issue.
- Follow our [Security Policy](SECURITY.md) for coordinated disclosure.
- Allow reasonable time for patches before public discussion.

---

## 5. Compliance & Enforcement

Violation of this policy may result in:

- Revocation of contributor privileges
- Removal from community platforms
- Reporting to relevant authorities, if illegal activity is involved
- Legal action in cases of willful harm or criminal conduct

---

## 6. Questions & Reporting

If you are unsure whether a particular use case is permitted, or to report a violation of this policy:

- Open a **private** GitHub Security Advisory
- Email the maintainers (see [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md))
